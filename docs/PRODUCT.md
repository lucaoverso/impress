# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The Suite Escolar is used by teachers, school coordinators, managers, and
administrators in day-to-day school operations.

Teachers often use it between classes and need to complete tasks quickly, such
as sending classroom activities for printing, booking equipment, submitting
documents, and checking their requests.

Coordinators and managers use it to record and follow up on pedagogical or
disciplinary occurrences, organize resources, review requests, and consult
management information.

Administrators configure users, permissions, quotas, printers, resources, and
other operational data. Their screens may contain more detail, but must remain
predictable and clearly organized.

## Product Purpose

The product brings recurring school services into one coherent system. It
should help users finish operational tasks with few steps, understandable
language, clear feedback, and protection against common mistakes.

Success means that a user can understand where to start and complete the
intended task without needing technical knowledge or formal training.

## Brand Personality

Clear, trustworthy, and intuitive.

The interface should feel calm and capable. It should apply established product
UI practices without becoming impersonal, overly technical, or decorative.
Language should be direct, supportive, and grounded in familiar school terms.

## Anti-references

The product must not resemble a server administration panel, a generic
enterprise dashboard, or a promotional landing page.

Avoid dense screens with competing actions, unexplained technical terminology,
decorative effects that distract from the task, inconsistent controls, and
workflows that require users to learn how the system is implemented.

Do not treat visual novelty as a substitute for usability. Familiar controls,
clear hierarchy, and consistent behavior are preferred over unusual
interactions.

## Design Principles

1. Lead users to one clear primary action on each screen.
2. Reveal complexity progressively and explain choices in school language.
3. Prevent mistakes with validation, availability feedback, summaries, and
   confirmation before consequential actions.
4. Keep patterns consistent across modules so learning one workflow helps with
   the next.
5. Design for real school conditions: limited time, varied technical
   confidence, and frequent use on mobile devices.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 Level AA.

All essential workflows must support keyboard navigation, visible focus,
readable contrast, semantic labels, and clear error identification. Status and
meaning must never depend on color alone. Motion must be purposeful and respect
`prefers-reduced-motion`.

Layouts and language should accommodate users with different levels of digital
confidence. Touch targets, responsive behavior, and plain Portuguese are
required for the primary workflows.
